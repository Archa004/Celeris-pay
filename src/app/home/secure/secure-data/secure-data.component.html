SCA only applies to processing payments from cards issued in Europe (including the UK) AND you are processing these payments through a European acquirer. If none or only one of the two statements applies to you SCA is not required, but should still be applied out of safety. However even if SCA does apply, certain payments processed may fall out of the requirement for SCA and may be exempted.

What are the exemptions for SCA?

There are a few circumstances wherein a user can be exempted from the flow based on the level of risk, amount, recurrence and the payment channel being used. Exempting users from SCA increases the chance of frictionless transactions and thus decreases dropouts while simultaneously allowing PSPs to strike the perfect balance between the convenience of payments and fraud reduction. There are several cases in which an exemption is possible which will be outlined below:

Low-Risk Transactions

SCA is not mandated when a PSP having in place effective risk analysis tools, assesses that the risk of fraud associated with a remote payment transaction is low. The Issuer has the ultimate say on whether SCA needs to be applied. SCA is only required when both the cardholder’s issuer as well as the merchant’s acquirer are located in the EEA region. If either of these parties is outside the EEA, the SCA regulation can be exempted.
